The Art of the Box

It's helpful to know that everything on a webpage is inside of a box. What do I mean by this? Well, for example, this text is inside a box that I created called "Article", which is inside a box labeled "Article Container", which is inside a box labeled "Webpage Container" which is inside a box labeled "Body". Are you confused yet? Because that would be normal, but we'll round this point out, I promise. I'm just going to use some non-traditional terminology to help you get there with me!

To help us get there together, I want you to imagine that you're building a house. In order to build that house, we'll need a builder, a painter, and an electrician. The builder frames out the house, the painter makes it look fun and interesting, and the electrician makes sure that when you flip the light switch, the light comes on!

  • Content
  • Padding
  • Border

Okay! Now that we've talked about padding, let's talk about margin! Padding and margin are going to be your two most useful tools when it comes to arranging things, but I'll admit. Even I sometimes get confused about which one to manipulate to make things work right!

Padding is the area inside a box, and margin is the area outside of a box. Margin, because it's usually unseen, is more like a forcefield around something that keeps other things at a distance that you decide.
